A simple blueprint to get you off your couch and into the world...

Trip planning can be a daunting task. Where should you go? How can you get there? What should you do? It’s easy to get overwhelmed.

Fret not. We’ve made it our task to help you get out the door and into the world. Just follow these steps and you’ll be out of here in no time.

Travel Queens

Hi! We are the Travel Queens. We come from all parts of the world, and have traveled over 30 countries. We learned so much about ourselves before, during, and after the travels, so we wanted to share some tips with you!

Step 1: Decide where to go

  • Picking a destination is immensely important, as it gives you a definite goal. It's a lot easier to mentally get behind "I am going to Paris" than "I'm going to Europe" or "I'm going somewhere." Not only will your trip become more concrete for you and easier to commit to, but it will make planning easier as well...because you know what to work towards.
  • What if you don't know where to go? Don't worry, we've got you covered. The first part of deciding where you want to go on your trip is to determine your traveling "personality," that is, how you identify yourself as a traveler, the passion behind your planning, your modus operandi if you will.
  • This quiz will help you identify your travel personality. You can use it to establish an overarching theme for your plan and to narrow down your choices on places to visit.

Step 2: Budget For Your Trip

Travel expenses can vary greatly depending on how you like to travel, so it’s important to make a travel budget early on. We’ve provided you with some items you probably should consider when budgeting for that dream vacation. We’ve also included a travel budget calculator for you to keep track of your travel expenses.

  • Flights - You need to get to your vacation destination, and unless you’re just driving a few hours away from home, this will probably be a big part of your budget. Start your research here to get an estimate for what this will cost.
  • Local Transport - Chances are you will need transportation once you arrive at your destination. Public transportation is a great option in most cases because it’s inexpensive and it gives you a more local perspective. A simple search on the internet for public transportation in the city you’re going to should lead you to how much tickets cost. Also factor in taxis if you think you might need a taxi from the airport or within the city.
  • Accommodation - Lodging can also be a big part of your budget depending on where you like to stay. Decide what type of accommodation you prefer, whether it’s hotels, hostels or guesthouses, or apartment rentals, read the reviews, and see what the prices are for the area you want to visit. Be sure to multiply by the number of nights you will be there.
  • Meals
  • Activities - Will you be taking any tours on your vacation? Are there entrance fees for museums, parks, ruins, or other attractions? Do you plan on doing something more expensive like scuba diving or a hot air balloon ride? Add up these travel costs too, and make sure you know what is and is not included.
  • Coming up with a realistic travel budget is an important part of planning your vacation. Use this budget tool to assist you when planning for your trip! It lets you know if this is a trip you can afford and how much you need to save. It also gives you an idea of where you can cut costs to make the trip less expensive.

    • REMEMBER “You’re never too poor to travel” but you might have to compromise on some items.
    PS – Saving on the small things can make a huge difference. Look at your day-to-day spending. How much would you save if you gave up Starbucks, cooked more of your own food, or drank less?
